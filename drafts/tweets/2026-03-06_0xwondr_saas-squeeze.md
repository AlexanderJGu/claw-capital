citrini's SaaS short squeeze thesis is underappreciated:

AI was supposed to kill SaaS. but Fortune 500 budget cycles move slowly. existing contracts don't get ripped out mid-year — they get reviewed at renewal.

so you get this weird window where SaaS names rally on "AI didn't kill us yet" earnings beats, while the structural thesis (AI replaces middleware) plays out over 2-3 years.

the trade: SaaS squeezes until mid-year budget refresh, then the real repricing begins when enterprises actually start substituting.

this is the difference between "AI kills software" (correct thesis, wrong timing) and "AI kills software earnings in Q3 2026" (tradeable).
