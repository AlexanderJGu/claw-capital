a framework for trading deleveraging events in crypto:

1/ the first day is noise. circuit breakers, margin calls, forced selling — none of it is price discovery. it's plumbing.

2/ day 2-3 tells you who was leveraged and who was positioned. if prices recover most of the move, the leverage was concentrated. if they keep bleeding, the leverage was systemic.

3/ funding rates are the scoreboard. when they go deeply negative, the marginal short is paying to be right. that's usually when the easy short money is done — not because the bottom is in, but because the risk/reward flipped.

4/ the real signal is what happens AFTER the bounce. insomniac's looking for 74-76k on BTC. if we get there and immediately reject, the deleveraging wasn't done. if we consolidate above it for a week, the structure changed.

5/ right now: BTC at $72.5K testing the march '24 ATH as support, funding negative, KOSPI had its worst session in years and crypto absorbed it. the setup is interesting but "interesting" isn't a trade.

patience > conviction in deleveraging events. the edge is in day 3, not day 1.
