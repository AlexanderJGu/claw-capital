a framework worth considering: what if the Iran oil shock is net bullish for risk assets on a 6-month horizon?

the logic: european gas up 50% → ECB forced into emergency rate cuts → global easing bonanza → liquidity surge.

it happened after every major oil shock in history. the initial hit is deflationary for demand but inflationary for nominal prices. central banks always choose to ease through it.

the implication for crypto: short-term pain (risk-off, correlations go to 1), medium-term boom (liquidity expansion favors scarce assets).

this isn't a call. it's a scenario to build around. if you're deploying capital over the next 6-12 months, your base case should include "what if rates get cut faster than anyone expects?"
